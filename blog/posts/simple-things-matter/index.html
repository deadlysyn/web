Simple Things Matter

dev git

Often in life we rush through things we’ve done so many times we feel they are simple. Some times they are simple, but in social contexts it is easy to forget how a little time taken to be intentional can help others (or ourselves) in the future.

One example of this I encounter almost daily is git commit messages. Often you will see one-liners such as “fixed typo” or “squashed bug” â€“ most likely meaningful enough to the person who just spent hours tracking down the typo or figuring out how to fix the bug. What about a few months later? Or at 3AM when you need to roll back?

I like to think quality commit messages are one of the best ways to document code’s evolution over time. This of course goes hand in hand with good commenting (explaining context vs behavior) and clean architecture/code.

Often folks look at me like I’m crazy (which I am, but perhaps not on this point)… This seems especially true on DevOps teams practicing Infrastructure as Code. When discussing “quality” in terms of commit messages, I often point to this gem. Recently I also stumbled across this newer incantation. They only take a few minutes to read, but adopting similar practices will almost certainly improve your project’s maintainability over time.
